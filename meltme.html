Melt Me



When she came to me, her wrist burning

with blood and salt water singeing her eyes,

I felt nothing. 

 

When he told me that it was a noose and not 

a heart attack that cremated our uncle,

I was empty.

 

I was the ice to their fire.

Nothing can extinguish the blue veins

that freeze my entire body over.



Just like nothing could stop that rabid spider

entrapped by the glory of a lamentable lighter

from baring its life into a suicide shot gun



and leaping into that wretched radiance

on that fiery summer day, when even the

moth had the sense to stay away.



I am but a stripped screw caught

in an elderly piece of wood, unable

to barrel as brilliantly as this forsaken bug.



How pathetic and miserable must I be

to envy an arachnid with a fatal fervor?

But it must be better than never feeling anything



but the numbing of your bones and your unthawed

heart passing hail through your nerves,

and you always wondered why you had a cold touch.
