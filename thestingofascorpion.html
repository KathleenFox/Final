The Sting of a Scorpion

I was trying to defeat the screeching scorpion

that pattered about the screen of my video game,

when I felt a chill so icy that trees began to whine

under the pressure of it. It raised roaming skin

on my neck to dance to the mutilated strings

of a soulless symphony.  



And I remembered your scarlet jacket,

so crumpled, that it looked like it had just

survived a tornado. It barely braced the wind

as you walked along, while I burst into song

in the middle of the frostbitten New York street.



You laughed without restraint and I stroked down

the street like a butterfly flying to a bohemian beat

and we were like peacocks beside Central Park 

and we laughed like clowns watching Carmina Burana,

taking the lyrics for what they were and not

what the opera snobs proclaimed they meant.



But the breeze passes and so does the memory,

as I watch the scorpion suffer and turn to scalding ash.

And I embraced that sultry sting

that comes with being alone. It devours

my mind like a surf that envelops a ship stuck in a storm.
