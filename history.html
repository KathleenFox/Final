History



One morning I lazily woke up feeling as cramped as a loaded

attic when the Christmas decorations are finally brought up again.

Yawns escaped from my lips like fireworks and I felt

a little misplaced in my queer bed shrouded in clothes

that no longer fit me and my walls plastered with awards

that no longer defined me. Sometimes I wonder



what makes me unique? What is it that makes my gears pulse

and blood rotate in a way that is different from the other billion

people on this planet, or even from the 38,871 denizens

of Manalapan, New Jersey? Two percent of one’s gene pool

 decides their phenotype, yet, for centuries that two percent

has been used to enforce, enslave, and empower



as discriminately as God. Two percent is all that

makes me, me, and not Bernadette Peters

or that homeless solider with a cracked cup on the edge

of a crowded highway. The body is a knight

and the mind is a king, but who in these frivolous fantasies

do we incline our indulgence. Mind over matter is a lie.



As much as we want to believe it is our trifling thoughts

and tactics that tell our history, our bountiful birth bodies

are the ones that decide who wins the trophy and gets to survive

in this Snow White world. But at the same time, my mind

is a pond, set off by anything from a duck’s foot or feathery

bomb and my body is the sullen shore, demented

by that pond’s wallowing waves and futile frogs,

both connected at the knavish core. Two hours ago I was

in low tide with only a fruit fly to perturb my sleep but now

I am sinking as a whale erupts from my sand floating

like a dead man. I am a changeling and I refuse

to be classified by my red bones or melancholic undertones.
